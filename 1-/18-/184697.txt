Hallo allerseits,


ich gehe gerade der Geschichte des Bungalow nach. Dazu
gibt es interessante Literatur.  Das Buch von Ihno O.
King "The Bungalow - Production of a Global Culture" gibt
zum Thema viel her. Er fuehrt Autoren an, die Hinweise
zu den Huetten geben, deren Bauweise von den Europaeer
in Indien uebernommen worden sein sollen. Ein Reisender
in Indien, Comte du Modave, der im 16. 01. 19. in Indien unter-
wegs war, beschreibt den "bangla". King zitiert ihn:

" 'A bangla is a pavilion of bamboo covered .. with thatch
or leaves from trees which one constructs for some special
occasion, like a marriage, a big fete, a meeting place du-
ring the hunt or simply a meeting place.' "  (1)

Im spaeten 19.Jahrhundert benannte man mit "bangla"
offensichtlich die Huette bengalischer Bauern. King fuehrt
aus:

"The 'bangla' was also used more permanently. In the later
nineteenth century, the term desribed the common hut of
the Bengal peasant, which had 'a sloping roof on two sides
and two gable ends' (H-J) It is probably this type of dwel-
ling which is described at greater length by Francis Bu-
chanan in 1810 (1838, ":922-4):
 'The style of private ediface that is proper and peculiar to
Bengal, consists of a hut with a pent roof constructed of
two sloping sides which meet in a ridge forming the seg-
ment of a circle so that it has a resemblance to a boat when
overturned... This kind of hut, it is said, from being peculiar
to Bengal, is called the native Banggolo... Among the na-
tives, the poor man has one hut for himself and cattle, the
richer men increase the number without altering the plan
of the building.' (F.Buchanan)
A wealthy Hindu familiy had a group of ten such huts, for
various purposes and family members, the average size
of each being about ten by eight cubits;* a 'common la-
bourer', with a wife and two children, had only one, eight
by six cubits." (2)

Zum Wort "banggolo" oder "Bungalow" fuehrt King an:

"Where the Frenchman preferred the local term, the Dutch
slightly adapted it, noting on one of their navigation charts
of the Hugli estuary, 'Bangaelaer op Speelhuys', apparently
referring to a place for rest or recreation (H-J)." (3)

Der lokale Begriff fuer diese leichten Huetten scheint
"bangla" gewesen zu sein. Die hollaendischen Seefahrer
haben daraus offenbar "Bangaelaer" gemacht.
