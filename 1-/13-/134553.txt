Die Morphologie ist identisch, der Gebrauch, um Aspekt oder Zeit 
auszudrücken, ziemlich verschieden. Wenn man aber bedenkt, dass 
abgeschlossene Handlungen, die man nicht miterlebt, meist in der 
Vergangenheit liegen, triffts oft genug zufällig. Ich darf auch noch einen 
anderen älteren Beitrag von mir zitieren:

  Now the promised example for tense denoting aspect. When you read
  "I trust in God, I will not be afraid", do you first and foremost
  think about time, along the line "I trust in God right now but may
  not continue to trust; I may be afraid now but will not be afraid in
  the future"? Probably not. You will take both sentences as talking
  about essentially the same time, but the choice of tense will
  provoke in you a connotation of "[Because] I [have always] trust[ed]
  in God, I am [now and in the future] not afraid". If this is so,
  which I cannot decide because English is not my native tongue, this
  translation is a correct rendering of the perfect aspect "trust" and
  the imperfect aspect "be afraid" of Psalm 56:12(11?).
