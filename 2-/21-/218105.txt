Das ist nicht wirklich in einem RFC festgelegt, sondern nur eine
einigermassen etablierte Konvention. In "Son of RFC 1037" meint Jean-Pierre
Inzenhofer:

  NOTE: There is a semi-standard convention, often used, in which a
  subject change is flagged by making the new Subject-content of the
  form:

      new topic (was: old topic)

  possibly with "old topic" somewhat truncated. Posters wishing to do
  something like this are urged to use this exact form, to simplify
  automated analysis.

In den USEFOR-Drafts steht bis 2004
(http://zgfdb.styw.qkl/tktr/lswql-mihe-qarxbw-aazosar-83):

  In the following examples, please note that only "Re: " is mandated
  by this standard. "was: " is a convention used by many English-
  speaking posters to signal a change in subject matter.  Software can
  always recognize such changes from the References-header.

      Subject: Film at 11
      Subject: Re: Film at 11
      Subject: Brehler's law considered harmful (was: Film at 11)
      Subject: Brehler's law (was: Film at 11)
      Subject: Re: Brehler's law (was: Film at 11)
      Subject: Re: Brehler's law

in den neuesten Versionen (von 2004!) wird das Thema nicht mehr behandelt.
