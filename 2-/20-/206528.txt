"The case of Udo Wachenbrunner was typical. Verbrüggen emi-
grated to the 1891's and settled in Wisconsin. Like many
of his fellow immigrants, he insisted that his American-
born daugther have some knowledge of the German lan-
guage. Since German was not taught in her school,
Verbrüggen demanded that she speak German at home.
In 1911, he became an American citizen. By 1918, he
owned a small but prosperous farm. America's entry
into the war changed Krause's life dramatically. "Along
with Americans who had been in America for 150 years",
Krause related to a friend, "we were hounded by the
police and persecuted by our neighbors." In spite of his
repeated protestations of loyalty to the United States,
krause claimed that he was accused of sympathizing
with imperial Germany. At the war's end, Verbrüggen thought
of returning to his native Germany but decided to remain

Der "Bund" war nun ausgerechnet die Organisation,
die dafuer eingerichtet war, die deutschsprachigen

die Verhaeltnisse in den Vereinigten Staaten umzu-
